# Tokyo Travel Guide

Tokyo, the capital of Japan, is a fascinating blend of ancient tradition and cutting-edge modernity. With a population of about 13.9 million people, it is the world's most populated metropolitan area.

## Understand
Tokyo has been a major urban center for nearly 400 years. It combines ultra-modern skyscrapers with historic temples and gardens. The city is clean, safe, and efficient with excellent public transportation.

## Get in
Tokyo is served by two major airports: Narita International Airport (NRT) and Haneda Airport (HND). Haneda is closer to the city center and more convenient.

## Get around
The Tokyo Metro is the best way to get around. Buses, taxis, and trains are abundant. Get a Suica or Pasmo card for easy payment on all public transport.

## See
- Tokyo Tower: 333m tall, offers panoramic views of the city
- Senso-ji Temple: Tokyo's oldest temple in the Asakusa district
- Meiji Shrine: Peaceful Shinto shrine surrounded by forest
- Shibuya Crossing: World's busiest pedestrian crossing
- Tokyo Skytree: Modern skyscraper with observation decks
- Tsukiji Outer Market: Famous fish and food market

## Do
- Visit themed entertainment districts
- Take a day trip to Mount Fuji
- Experience traditional tea ceremony
- Attend a sumo wrestling match
- Visit anime and manga shops in Akihabara

## Eat
Tokyo has incredible food culture. Try ramen, sushi, tempura, tonkatsu, and okonomiyaki. Street food is excellent and affordable.

## Budget
A 3-day trip can cost $600-1200 depending on accommodation and dining preferences.
