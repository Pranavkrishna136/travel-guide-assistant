# Madrid Travel Guide

Madrid, the capital of Spain, is known for world-class museums, vibrant nightlife, and delicious cuisine.

## Understand
Madrid is Spain's political and cultural center. The city has excellent museums, lively neighborhoods, and a strong café culture. It's a city for art lovers and food enthusiasts.

## Get in
Adolfo Suárez Madrid-Barajas Airport is the main gateway. Trains connect Madrid to all of Spain and Europe. Bus connections available.

## Get around
The metro system is excellent and affordable. Buses cover the entire city. Taxis available. Walking is wonderful in many areas.

## See
- Prado Museum: One of the world's finest art museums
- Royal Palace: Official residence of the Spanish monarch
- Retiro Park: Large urban park with lake and museum
- Reina Sofía Museum: Modern art museum with Picasso's Guernica
- Templo de Debod: Egyptian temple from antiquity
- Plaza Mayor: Historic central square
- Puerta del Sol: Historic gate and central meeting point

## Do
- Explore major museums
- Relax in Retiro Park
- Experience tapas and wine bars
- Enjoy flamenco shows
- Shop at local markets
- Visit nearby Toledo or Segovia

## Eat
Spanish cuisine features tapas, paella, churros, jamón ibérico, and seafood. Wine is excellent. Café culture strong. Street food abundant.

## Budget
A 3-day trip can cost $500-1000.
