# Venice Travel Guide

Venice, built on islands in a lagoon, is one of the most unique and romantic cities in the world.

## Understand
Venice has no roads—only canals! The city is built on pilings over water. It's famous for gondolas, art, and architecture. Venice is a UNESCO World Heritage site.

## Get in
Marco Polo Airport serves Venice. Buses, trains, and boats connect to mainland. Water transport is essential within Venice.

## Get around
No cars—only boats, gondolas, and walking! Vaporetto (water buses) are the main public transport. Walking through narrow streets is the primary way to explore.

## See
- St. Mark's Basilica: Byzantine church with stunning mosaics
- Doge's Palace: Historic seat of Venice's government
- Grand Canal: Main waterway through the city
- Rialto Bridge: Famous arched bridge crossing the Grand Canal
- Peggy Guggenheim Collection: Modern art museum
- Basilica di Santa Maria della Salute: Historic church
- San Giorgio Maggiore: Church on island with bell tower

## Do
- Take gondola rides through canals
- Visit museums and galleries
- Cross all bridges
- Explore nearby islands (Murano, Burano)
- Enjoy sunset boat cruises
- Photography walks

## Eat
Venetian cuisine features seafood heavily. Try risotto nero, spaghetti alle vongole, fritto misto, and tiramisu. Wine from Veneto region excellent.

## Budget
A 3-day trip can cost $700-1400.
