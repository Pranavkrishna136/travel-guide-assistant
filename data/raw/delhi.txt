# Delhi Travel Guide

Delhi, India's capital, is a bustling metropolis blending ancient history with modernity. It's the heart of Indian politics, culture, and commerce.

## Understand
Delhi is divided into Old Delhi and New Delhi. Old Delhi is chaotic and historic with narrow streets. New Delhi is more organized and colonial in architecture. The city is intense, vibrant, and energetic.

## Get in
Indira Gandhi International Airport (IGI) is the main gateway. Trains connect to all of India. Buses available for regional travel.

## Get around
Local trains and metro are lifelines. Auto-rickshaws are cheap and everywhere. Buses crowded. Walking in New Delhi is pleasant.

## See
- Red Fort: Historic Mughal fort in Old Delhi
- Jama Masjid: One of India's largest mosques
- India Gate: War memorial and iconic landmark
- Rashtrapati Bhavan: Presidential residence
- Humayun's Tomb: Mughal tomb, UNESCO site
- Chandni Chowk: Historic street in Old Delhi
- Connaught Place: Colonial-era shopping area
- Lotus Temple: Bahai temple, architectural marvel

## Do
- Explore Old Delhi streets
- Visit temples and mosques
- Experience street food culture
- Shop at markets
- Visit museums
- Enjoy evening walks
- Take cooking classes

## Eat
Delhi street food is legendary. Try chole bhature, butter chicken, dal makhani, samosas, and chaats. Street vendors everywhere. Diverse cuisines available.

## Budget
A 3-day trip can cost $150-300.
