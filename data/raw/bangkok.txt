# Bangkok Travel Guide

Bangkok, the capital of Thailand, is a vibrant, chaotic, and energetic city known for its temples, street food, and nightlife.

## Understand
Bangkok is Thailand's largest city and home to over 10 million people. It's a blend of sacred temples, royal palaces, and modern skyscrapers. The city has a relaxed vibe despite being densely populated.

## Get in
Suvarnabhumi Airport and Don Mueang Airport serve Bangkok. Both are well-connected to the city. Trains and buses also connect to neighboring countries.

## Get around
The BTS Skytrain and MRT Metro are the fastest ways to travel. Taxis and tuk-tuks are cheap and abundant. River boats are a scenic alternative.

## See
- Grand Palace: Royal residence with stunning Thai architecture
- Wat Phra Kaew: Temple housing the Emerald Buddha
- Wat Arun: Beautiful temple on the Chao Phraya River
- Floating Markets: Traditional markets on canals and rivers
- Jim Thompson House: Historic Thai teak house museum
- Lumpini Park: Green space with jogging paths and lake
- Chinatown: Bustling market area with street food

## Do
- Take a long-tail boat tour of canals and floating markets
- Get a traditional Thai massage
- Explore night markets and street food stalls
- Visit Muay Thai boxing matches
- Shop at night bazaars

## Eat
Bangkok is a street food paradise. Try pad thai, pad see ew, green curry, tom yum soup, and mango sticky rice. Everything is fresh and extremely affordable.

## Budget
A 3-day trip can cost $300-700.
