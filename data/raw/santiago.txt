# Santiago Travel Guide

Santiago, Chile's capital, is known for Andes views, wine, and modern infrastructure.

## Basic Travel Information
Santiago is a modern, developed city nestled between mountains. Chile's economic and cultural center. Gateway to the Atacama Desert and Patagonia.

## Tourist Attractions
- San Crist√≥bal Hill: Hilltop park with views and virgin statue
- Palacio de La Moneda: Presidential palace
- National Museum of Fine Arts: Important art collection
- Pre-Columbian Art Museum: Ancient artifacts
- Lastarria Neighborhood: Bohemian arts district
- Vitacura: Upscale neighborhood with restaurants
- Mapocho Market: Historic market
- Maipo Valley: Wine region nearby

## Best Time to Visit
- September-November: Spring, mild weather
- December-February: Summer, warm
- March-May: Autumn, pleasant
- June-August: Winter, cool but dry

## Transportation Options
- Metro system: Modern, extensive, affordable
- Buses: Cheap but crowded
- Taxis and ride-sharing apps
- Walking in central areas
- Commuter trains

## Cultural Information
- Developed, European-influenced
- Wine culture important
- Passionate about football
- Artistic and intellectual scene
- Friendly and educated population
- Indigenous heritage present

## Safety Guidelines
- Generally safe compared to region
- Main areas secure
- Avoid certain neighborhoods at night
- Use official taxis or apps
- Watch belongings in markets
- Political demonstrations possible
- Overall low crime in tourist areas

## Dining
- Chilean wine world-class
- Fresh seafood and meat
- Pastel de choclo (corn pie)
- Empanadas and traditional dishes
- Street food and restaurants
- Coffee culture growing

## Budget
A 3-day trip costs $400-800.
