# Beijing Travel Guide

Beijing, China's capital, is one of the world's oldest cities and home to iconic historical sites.

## Understand
Beijing is a blend of ancient history and modern China. It's home to imperial palaces, temples, and the Great Wall. The city is massive, organized, and growing rapidly.

## Get in
Beijing Capital International Airport and Daxing International Airport serve the city. High-speed trains connect to other Chinese cities. Buses available.

## Get around
The metro system is extensive and cheap. Buses are comprehensive. Taxis and ride-sharing available. Walking in certain areas.

## See
- Great Wall of China: Ancient defensive structure, UNESCO site
- Forbidden City: Imperial palace, UNESCO site
- Temple of Heaven: Sacrificial temple, UNESCO site
- Summer Palace: Royal retreat, UNESCO site
- Ming Tombs: Burial site of Ming emperors
- Tiananmen Square: Large central square
- National Palace Museum: Imperial collections
- Hutongs: Traditional narrow alleys

## Do
- Hike the Great Wall
- Explore imperial sites
- Visit temples
- Experience hutong neighborhoods
- Enjoy traditional tea ceremonies
- Visit museums
- Photography tours

## Eat
Beijing cuisine is famous. Try Peking duck, dumplings, noodles, and hot pot. Street food abundant. Tea culture strong. Restaurants diverse.

## Budget
A 3-day trip can cost $300-600.
