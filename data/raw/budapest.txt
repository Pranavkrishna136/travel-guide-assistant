# Budapest Travel Guide

Budapest, Hungary's capital, is known for thermal baths, Danube views, and Art Nouveau architecture.

## Understand
Budapest spans the Danube River with Buda (hills) and Pest (plains). The city is famous for relaxation in spas and romantic ambiance. It's affordable and welcoming.

## Get in
Ferenc Liszt International Airport is the main gateway. Trains connect to Europe. Buses available. Danube cruises from Austria.

## Get around
Metro, tram, and bus system is excellent. Walking along Danube scenic. Taxis and ride-sharing apps available.

## See
- Buda Castle: Royal castle with views
- Fisherman's Bastion: Terrace with Danube views
- Széchenyi Thermal Bath: Famous thermal bath complex
- Gellért Hill: Hill with views and monuments
- Parliament Building: Impressive Gothic Revival building
- Great Synagogue: Europe's largest synagogue
- Danube River: Scenic river
- St. Stephen's Basilica: Historic basilica

## Do
- Relax in thermal baths
- Walk across Danube bridges
- Visit historic sites
- Danube river cruises
- Photography walks
- Enjoy wine and food
- Experience nightlife

## Eat
Hungarian cuisine is hearty and spicy. Try goulash, pörkölt, langos, and chimney cake. Paprika is essential ingredient. Wine excellent. Street food abundant.

## Budget
A 3-day trip can cost $300-600.
