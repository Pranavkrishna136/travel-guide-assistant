# Hong Kong Travel Guide

Hong Kong, a special administrative region of China, is known for skyscrapers, harbor, and dim sum.

## Understand
Hong Kong is a global financial hub. The city blends Chinese and Western cultures. It's ultra-modern with efficient infrastructure.

## Get in
Hong Kong International Airport is the main gateway. High-speed rail to mainland China. Ferries to nearby islands.

## Get around
The MTR (metro) system is world-class. Trams, buses, and ferries available. Taxis abundant. Walking in many areas.

## See
- Victoria Peak: Highest point with views
- Star Ferry: Historic ferry with harbor views
- Central: Business district with history
- Temple Street Night Market: Busy night market
- Man Mo Temple: Historic Chinese temple
- Tian Tan Buddha: Giant bronze statue on Lantau Island
- Stanley Beach: Beach on south side
- Causeway Bay: Shopping district

## Do
- Peak tramway rides and views
- Harbor cruises
- Shopping on multiple islands
- Temple and shrine visits
- Hiking trails
- Beach activities
- Experience dim sum

## Eat
Cantonese cuisine is world-famous. Try dim sum, roasted duck, seafood, and noodles. Street food abundant. Restaurants diverse and excellent.

## Budget
A 3-day trip can cost $400-800.
