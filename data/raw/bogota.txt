# Bogotá Travel Guide

Bogotá, Colombia's capital, is known for museums, high altitude, and cultural renaissance.

## Basic Travel Information
Bogotá sits at high altitude (2,640m) in the Andes. Colombia's political and cultural center. Experiencing cultural and economic renaissance after troubled past.

## Tourist Attractions
- Gold Museum: World's largest gold collection
- Monserrate: Mountain sanctuary with views
- Historic La Candelaria: Colonial neighborhood
- Colombian National Museum: History and culture
- Botero Museum: Fernando Botero's artwork
- Plaza Bolívar: Historic central plaza
- Usaquén: Hip neighborhood with street market
- Zipaquirá Salt Cathedral: Salt mine cathedral (day trip)

## Best Time to Visit
- December-January: Dry season
- July-August: Dry season
- Year-round good (temperate highland climate)
- Avoid rainy seasons

## Transportation Options
- Metro system: Modern and expanding
- TransMilenio: Bus rapid transit system
- Buses and minibuses
- Taxis and ride-sharing apps
- Walking in safe areas

## Cultural Information
- Emerging cultural scene
- Passion for literature and art
- Indigenous heritage
- Musical traditions
- Warm and hospitable people
- Spanish colonial legacy

## Safety Guidelines
- Improved significantly in recent years
- Tourist areas generally safe
- Avoid certain neighborhoods
- Use official taxis or apps
- Don't travel at night alone
- High altitude, need acclimatization
- Overall improving security

## Dining
- Colombian coffee excellent
- Fresh tropical fruits
- Ajiaco (potato soup) traditional
- Grilled meat popular
- Street food and restaurants
- Traditional Colombian cuisine

## Budget
A 3-day trip costs $200-400.
