# Manila Travel Guide

Manila, Philippines' capital, is known for historic sites, nightlife, and beach access.

## Understand
Manila is the heart of the Philippines. The city is chaotic, energetic, and warm. It's a mix of colonial architecture and modern development.

## Get in
Ninoy Aquino International Airport is the main gateway. Ferries to nearby islands. Buses and jeepneys to other regions.

## Get around
Jeepneys (public transport) are iconic. Metro rail limited. Taxis and ride-sharing apps. Walking in Intramuros.

## See
- Intramuros: Historic walled city
- Manila Cathedral: Gothic cathedral
- Fort Santiago: Historic fortress
- Rizal Park: Large urban park with monuments
- Binondo: Oldest Chinatown in the world
- National Museum: History and art
- Baywalk: Seaside promenade
- Makati: Modern business district

## Do
- Explore historic Intramuros
- Visit museums
- Enjoy nightlife
- Visit nearby Boracay or Palawan
- Experience street food
- Photography walks
- Relax at malls and restaurants

## Eat
Filipino cuisine is hearty and flavorful. Try adobo, lumpia, sinigang, fried chicken, and desserts. Street food abundant. Karaoke culture strong.

## Budget
A 3-day trip can cost $200-400.
