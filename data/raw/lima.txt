# Lima Travel Guide

Lima, Peru's capital, is known for culinary excellence, colonial architecture, and Incan history access.

## Basic Travel Information
Lima is Peru's largest city and culinary capital of South America. Blend of colonial and modern architecture. Gateway to Machu Picchu and other Peruvian sites.

## Tourist Attractions
- Historic Centre: Colonial buildings and plazas (UNESCO)
- Plaza Mayor: Main square with government buildings
- Museum of Peruvian Art: Extensive art collection
- Gold Museum: Peruvian gold artifacts
- Miraflores District: Upscale neighborhood with views
- Barranco District: Bohemian neighborhood with art
- San Isidro: Upscale residential area
- Pachacamac: Archaeological site outside city

## Best Time to Visit
- December-March: Summer, warmest and driest
- April-October: Winter, cool but clearer skies
- May-September: Best for Machu Picchu visits
- Avoid rainy season in Andes

## Transportation Options
- Metro system being expanded
- Buses and minibuses (cheap but chaotic)
- Taxis and ride-sharing apps
- Walking in tourist areas
- Trains to other regions

## Cultural Information
- World-renowned gastronomy
- Indigenous heritage and Incan history
- Spanish colonial influence
- Artistic and creative scene
- Warm, welcoming people
- Strong tradition of family

## Safety Guidelines
- Tourist areas generally safe
- Avoid certain neighborhoods after dark
- Don't use unmarked taxis
- Watch belongings in crowded areas
- Petty theft common in markets
- Robbery can occur in poor areas
- Stay aware of surroundings

## Dining
- World's best gastronomy
- Fresh seafood (ceviche especially)
- Traditional Peruvian dishes
- Guinea pig (cuy) specialty
- Potatoes in many forms
- Street food abundant and good

## Budget
A 3-day trip costs $250-500.
