# Amsterdam Travel Guide

Amsterdam, the capital of the Netherlands, is famous for its canals, bicycles, museums, and liberal culture.

## Understand
Amsterdam is a compact, bike-friendly city with a rich history as a trading port. It's known for its picturesque canals, historic architecture, and progressive values. The city is very walkable and pedestrian-friendly.

## Get in
Amsterdam Airport Schiphol is the main airport. Trains connect to other European cities. The city is accessible by road as well.

## Get around
Bicycles are the primary mode of transport - rent one to experience the city like locals. The tram network is excellent. Canals can be explored by boat tours.

## See
- Anne Frank House: Historic house-museum of Anne Frank
- Van Gogh Museum: World's largest Van Gogh collection
- Rijksmuseum: National museum with Dutch art and history
- Canal Ring: UNESCO-listed network of historic canals
- Dam Square: Central square with historic buildings
- Vondelpark: Large urban park with museums and cafes
- Windmills at Kinderdijk: Historic windmills outside Amsterdam

## Do
- Take a canal boat tour
- Cycle around the city
- Visit museums and art galleries
- Enjoy Dutch pancakes and stroopwafels
- Explore the Red Light District (cultural interest)
- Visit the Anne Frank House

## Eat
Dutch cuisine features cheese, fish, and hearty stews. Try stroopwafels, poffertjes, kibbeling (fried fish), and Dutch pancakes. Vegetarian options are abundant.

## Budget
A 3-day trip can cost $600-1200.
