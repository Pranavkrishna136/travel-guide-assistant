# Nairobi Travel Guide

Nairobi, Kenya's capital, is known as the gateway to African wildlife and safari adventures.

## Basic Travel Information
Nairobi is East Africa's largest city and economic hub. Modern infrastructure combined with African culture. Starting point for Kenyan safaris.

## Tourist Attractions
- Nairobi National Park: Wildlife in city view
- David Sheldrick Wildlife Trust: Elephant orphanage
- Giraffe Centre: Endangered giraffes up close
- Karen Blixen Museum: Author's historic home
- Nairobi Museum: Natural history and culture
- Nairobi National Museum: Anthropology and art
- Kibera: Largest urban slum (tours available)
- Ngong Hills: Hiking with views

## Best Time to Visit
- June-October: Dry season, wildlife abundant
- November-December: Short rains
- January-March: Long rains
- Year-round possible

## Transportation Options
- Matatus: Minibuses (crowded, chaotic)
- Uber and ride-sharing apps
- Buses and coach services
- Taxis (use regulated ones)
- Walking limited to safe areas

## Cultural Information
- African wildlife and conservation focus
- Diverse ethnic groups and traditions
- Growing tech industry (Silicon Savanna)
- Strong entrepreneurial culture
- Music and arts scene
- Warm and welcoming people

## Safety Guidelines
- Exercise caution in certain areas
- Tourist areas relatively secure
- Avoid large amounts of cash
- Use authorized taxis or apps
- Petty theft and robbery possible
- Carjacking can occur
- Avoid nighttime travel alone
- Respect local customs and dress

## Dining
- Nyama choma (grilled meat) famous
- Ugali and sukuma wiki (greens)
- Chapati (flatbread) common
- Fresh fruit abundant
- Indian and international cuisine
- Street food available

## Budget
A 3-day trip costs $300-600.
