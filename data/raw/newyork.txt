# New York City Travel Guide

New York City, often referred to as the Big Apple, is the most populous city in the United States and a global center for art, fashion, finance, and entertainment.

## Understand
NYC is divided into five boroughs: Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, The Bronx, and Staten Island. Manhattan is the most famous, home to iconic landmarks and business districts. The city never sleeps with activities available 24/7.

## Get in
Three major airports serve NYC: JFK, LaGuardia (LGA), and Newark (EWR). The subway and trains connect these to the city center.

## Get around
The NYC Subway is the fastest way to travel. Buses are also available. Get a MetroCard for unlimited travel. Walking is great for exploring neighborhoods.

## See
- Statue of Liberty: Iconic monument on Liberty Island
- Empire State Building: Historic Art Deco skyscraper with observation deck
- Central Park: 843-acre urban park with lakes, forests, and trails
- Times Square: Bright, bustling entertainment district
- Brooklyn Bridge: Historic suspension bridge with pedestrian walkway
- Metropolitan Museum of Art: One of the world's largest art museums
- One World Trade Center: Modern building with observation deck

## Do
- Watch a Broadway show
- Visit world-class museums
- Explore diverse neighborhoods: Chinatown, Little Italy, Greenwich Village
- Take a ferry to Staten Island
- Enjoy rooftop bars and dining

## Eat
NYC has incredible international cuisine. Try pizza, bagels, hot dogs, Cuban sandwiches, and authentic Asian cuisine. Food trucks are everywhere.

## Budget
A 3-day trip can cost $900-1500 for tourists.
