# Lisbon Travel Guide

Lisbon, Portugal's capital, is known for historic neighborhoods, tiles, and hilltop views.

## Understand
Lisbon is one of Europe's oldest cities perched on hills. The city is picturesque with colorful tiles, narrow streets, and friendly locals. It's more affordable than many European capitals.

## Get in
Humberto Delgado Airport is the main gateway. Trains connect to Europe. Buses available.

## Get around
Trams and metro are iconic and cheap. Buses available. Taxis and ride-sharing apps. Walking uphill is challenging but scenic.

## See
- Belém Tower: Historic tower by river, UNESCO site
- Jerónimos Monastery: Ornate monastery, UNESCO site
- Statue of Christ the King: Iconic statue on hill
- São Jorge Castle: Medieval castle with views
- Livraria Jello: Historic bookstore
- Azulejo Museum: Tile museum
- Pastéis de Nata Museum: History of pastry
- Tavares Restaurant: Historic restaurant

## Do
- Ride historic trams
- Explore neighborhoods
- Visit historic sites
- Enjoy pastéis de nata and wine
- Photography walks
- Visit nearby Cascais and Sintra
- Relax at cafes

## Eat
Portuguese cuisine features seafood and bread. Try bacalhau à Brás, cataplana seafood stew, and pastéis de nata. Wine and port excellent. Street food abundant.

## Budget
A 3-day trip can cost $400-800.
