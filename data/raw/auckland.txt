# Auckland Travel Guide

Auckland, New Zealand's largest city, is known for harbors, islands, outdoor activities, and cosmopolitan lifestyle.

## Understand
Auckland is set between two harbors. The city blends urban culture with access to nature. Outdoor activities are abundant and accessible.

## Get in
Auckland International Airport is the main gateway. Ferries to nearby islands. Buses and trains to other NZ cities.

## Get around
Public transport includes buses, ferries, and trains. Walking great in city center. Car rental for exploring. Ferries scenic to islands.

## See
- Sky Tower: Tallest structure with observation deck
- Waitemata Harbour: Beautiful harbor with yachts
- Mission Bay: Seaside promenade with beaches
- Rangitoto Island: Volcanic island with views
- War Memorial Museum: NZ history and Maori artifacts
- Auckland Domain: Large park with views
- Muriwai Gannet Colony: Seabird viewing

## Do
- Harbor cruises
- Island hopping
- Hiking and walking
- Water sports and beaches
- Visit museums
- Wine tasting nearby
- Experience Maori culture

## Eat
New Zealand cuisine features lamb, seafood, and fresh produce. Try pavlova, meat pies, fish and chips, and Maori hangi. Cafe culture strong.

## Budget
A 3-day trip can cost $600-1200.
