# London Travel Guide

London, the capital of the United Kingdom, is one of Europe's largest and most influential cities. It combines medieval buildings with ultra-modern architecture, historic landmarks with vibrant culture.

## Understand
London has been a major settlement for almost two millennia. The city is known for its British traditions, royalty, museums, and historic sites. It's a multicultural city with diverse neighborhoods.

## Get in
London has six major airports. Heathrow is the main international hub. The Tube (Underground), trains, and buses connect airports to the city.

## Get around
The London Underground (the Tube) is the fastest way to travel. Buses cover the entire city. Get an Oyster card or use contactless payment. Walking is wonderful for exploring.

## See
- Big Ben & Houses of Parliament: Iconic Gothic architecture
- Tower of London: Historic fortress with Crown Jewels
- Buckingham Palace: Official residence of the monarch
- Tower Bridge: Famous bascule bridge over the Thames
- Westminster Abbey: Historic church and burial place of monarchs
- British Museum: World's largest museum with millions of artifacts
- London Eye: Giant observation wheel with panoramic views

## Do
- Watch the Changing of the Guard at Buckingham Palace
- Take a Thames river cruise
- Explore historic markets: Borough Market, Camden Market
- Visit art galleries and theaters
- Shop on Oxford Street and Bond Street

## Eat
London has international cuisine from all over the world. Try fish and chips, traditional British breakfast, Indian curry, and afternoon tea. Street food markets are excellent.

## Budget
A 3-day trip can cost $700-1400 depending on dining and activities.
