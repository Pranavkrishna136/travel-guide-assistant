# Jerusalem Travel Guide

Jerusalem, contested holy city, is significant to Judaism, Christianity, and Islam.

## Basic Travel Information
Jerusalem is one of the world's oldest cities. Sacred to three major religions. Divided between Israel and Palestine with complex history and politics.

## Tourist Attractions
- Western Wall: Holiest site in Judaism
- Church of the Holy Sepulchre: Christian holy site
- Dome of the Rock: Islamic shrine
- Old City: UNESCO World Heritage Site with walls
- Temple Mount: Most sacred in Judaism
- Mount of Olives: Overlooks Old City
- Israel Museum: History and art
- Holocaust Memorial Museum: Yad Vashem

## Best Time to Visit
- March-May: Spring, pleasant weather
- September-October: Fall, mild weather
- June-August: Hot and dry
- December-January: Cool but manageable

## Transportation Options
- Buses: Main public transport
- Taxis and ride-sharing apps
- Walking in Old City (necessary)
- Light rail system expanding
- Minibuses/sherut between cities

## Cultural Information
- Three major religions center
- Complex political situation
- Rich history spanning millennia
- Diverse populations
- Strong traditions and customs
- Artistic and intellectual heritage

## Safety Guidelines
- Overall safe for tourists in main areas
- Avoid political demonstrations
- Security checkpoints common
- Respect religious sites and customs
- Don't photograph certain areas
- Tensions between communities possible
- Check current situation before visiting

## Dining
- Mediterranean and Middle Eastern cuisine
- Hummus, falafel, kebabs
- Fresh produce markets
- Israeli and Palestinian restaurants
- Street food varied
- Kosher restaurants in Jewish areas

## Budget
A 3-day trip costs $300-600.
