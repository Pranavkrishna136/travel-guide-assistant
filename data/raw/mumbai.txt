# Mumbai Travel Guide

Mumbai, formerly Bombay, is India's largest city and financial center. Known for Bollywood, contrasts, and vibrant street life.

## Understand
Mumbai is the heart of Indian cinema (Bollywood). The city is dense, chaotic, and energetic. It blends colonial architecture with modern buildings.

## Get in
Indira Gandhi International Airport (Delhi) and Mumbai International Airport serve the region. Trains connect to all of India. Buses available.

## Get around
Local trains are the lifeline of Mumbai. Buses are cheap but crowded. Taxis and auto-rickshaws available. Walking in certain areas.

## See
- Gateway of India: Historic monument and waterfront
- Marine Drive: Coastal road with Art Deco buildings
- Taj Mahal Palace Hotel: Iconic luxury hotel
- Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus: Historic railway station
- Bollywood Studios: Tours of film production facilities
- Haji Ali Mosque: Mosque on island in Arabian Sea
- Street markets and neighborhoods

## Do
- Experience street food culture
- Visit Bollywood studios
- Take boat rides to islands
- Explore markets and bazaars
- Enjoy nightlife and clubs
- Visit nearby beaches

## Eat
Mumbai street food is legendary. Try vada pav, pav bhaji, pani puri, and dosa. Chaat snacks popular. Indian cuisine diverse. Chai (tea) everywhere.

## Budget
A 3-day trip can cost $200-400.
