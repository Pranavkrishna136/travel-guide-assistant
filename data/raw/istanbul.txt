# Istanbul Travel Guide

Istanbul, straddling Europe and Asia, is Turkey's largest city and a major cultural hub. The only city spanning two continents, Istanbul bridges East and West.

## Understand
Istanbul has been conquered and renamed multiple times. It was Constantinople, then Istanbul. The city blends Ottoman, Byzantine, and modern cultures. It's a gateway between Europe and Asia.

## Get in
Istanbul Airport and Sabiha Gökçen Airport serve the city. Ferries connect Istanbul to other Turkish and Greek cities. Train and bus connections exist.

## Get around
The tram, metro, and bus system is efficient. Ferries across the Golden Horn and Bosphorus are scenic and fun. Taxis and dolmuş (shared minibuses) are common.

## See
- Blue Mosque: Ottoman mosque with blue tiles, UNESCO site
- Hagia Sophia: Historic Byzantine church turned mosque
- Topkapi Palace: Former royal residence with museums
- Grand Bazaar: Massive covered market with thousands of shops
- Galata Tower: Medieval tower with city views
- Bosphorus Strait: Waterway separating Europe and Asia
- Galata Bridge: Historic bridge with fishing and restaurants

## Do
- Take a Bosphorus cruise
- Shop in the Grand Bazaar
- Explore neighborhoods like Balat
- Get a Turkish bath (hammam) experience
- Try whirling dervish ceremony

## Eat
Turkish cuisine is diverse and delicious. Try kebab, meze, baklava, Turkish delight, and Turkish tea/coffee. Street food is excellent and affordable.

## Budget
A 3-day trip can cost $400-800.
