# Lagos Travel Guide

Lagos, Nigeria's largest city, is known as Africa's economic powerhouse and entertainment hub.

## Basic Travel Information
Lagos is Nigeria's largest city and West Africa's economic center. Vibrant, energetic, and fast-growing. One of Africa's most influential cities.

## Tourist Attractions
- Lekki Conservation Centre: Wildlife and nature
- Nike Art Gallery: Contemporary African art
- Oshodi Market: Busy traditional market
- Elegushi Beach: Popular beach
- Victoria Island: Upscale residential area
- Tarkwa Bay: Beach community
- Lagos Museum: History and culture
- Aje Market: Colorful traditional market

## Best Time to Visit
- November-February: Dry season, cooler
- March-October: Rainy season
- Dry season more comfortable for visiting

## Transportation Options
- BRT buses: Rapid transit system
- Taxis (yellow and black)
- Uber and ride-sharing apps
- Ferries: Scenic and useful
- Minibuses/danfo (chaotic)
- Walking limited (unsafe in some areas)

## Cultural Information
- Vibrant music and entertainment scene
- Nollywood film industry strong
- Fashion and designer culture
- Entrepreneurial and innovative
- Diverse ethnic groups
- Strong family and community values

## Safety Guidelines
- Exercise caution in certain areas
- Tourist areas relatively safe
- Use registered taxis or apps
- Avoid traveling at night alone
- Petty theft and robbery possible
- Congestion and traffic safety concerns
- Respect local customs
- Corruption and unofficial fees possible

## Dining
- Jollof rice famous
- Pepper soups and stews
- Fresh seafood abundant
- Traditional Nigerian dishes
- Street food (be cautious)
- Growing restaurant scene

## Budget
A 3-day trip costs $300-600.
