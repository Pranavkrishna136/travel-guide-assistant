# Shanghai Travel Guide

Shanghai, China's largest city, is a modern metropolis known for skyscrapers, shopping, and nightlife.

## Understand
Shanghai is China's economic center and a global financial hub. The city blends colonial architecture with ultra-modern buildings. It's cosmopolitan and bustling.

## Get in
Shanghai Pudong International Airport and Hongqiao International Airport serve the city. High-speed trains to other cities. Buses available.

## Get around
The metro system is extensive. Buses are cheap. Taxis abundant. Ride-sharing apps available. Walking in certain districts.

## See
- Oriental Pearl Tower: Iconic TV tower with views
- The Bund: Historic waterfront with colonial buildings
- Yu Garden: Classical Chinese garden
- City God Temple: Historic temple in garden
- Jing'an Temple: Buddhist temple in city center
- Shanghai Museum: World-class museum
- French Concession: Historic neighborhood
- Nanjing Road: Shopping street

## Do
- Explore historic Bund
- Shop and dine
- Visit temples and gardens
- Enjoy nightlife
- Take river cruises
- Visit museums
- Photography of skyline

## Eat
Shanghai cuisine features seafood and local dishes. Try xiaolongbao (soup dumplings), fried eel, hairy crabs, and noodles. Street food abundant.

## Budget
A 3-day trip can cost $400-800.
