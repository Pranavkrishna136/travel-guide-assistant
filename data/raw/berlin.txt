# Berlin Travel Guide

Berlin, the capital of Germany, is known for its history, art, and vibrant nightlife. A city of contrasts with both old and new architecture.

## Understand
Berlin is divided by the Berlin Wall during the Cold War, now a symbol of reunification. The city is culturally diverse and liberal. It's a hub for art, music, and alternative culture.

## Get in
Berlin Brandenburg Airport is the main airport. Trains connect Berlin to all of Europe efficiently. Bus connections available.

## Get around
The U-Bahn (metro) and S-Bahn (regional trains) are efficient. Trams and buses cover the city. Biking is popular and safe. Walking is great.

## See
- Brandenburg Gate: Iconic symbol of reunification
- Berlin Wall Memorial: Historic remains and museum
- Reichstag: Parliament building with glass dome
- Museum Island: Collection of five world-class museums
- East Side Gallery: Longest remaining wall section with art
- Checkpoint Charlie: Famous Cold War crossing point
- Charlottenburg Palace: Historic royal palace

## Do
- Explore museum collections
- Visit nightclubs and music venues
- Bike through the city
- Enjoy street art and graffiti
- Visit historic sites
- Experience alternative culture

## Eat
German cuisine includes schnitzel, currywurst, sausages, and pretzels. International cuisine abundant. Beer culture strong. Restaurants and street food diverse.

## Budget
A 3-day trip can cost $500-1000.
