# Los Angeles Travel Guide

Los Angeles, the second-largest city in the USA, is known for entertainment, beaches, and diverse neighborhoods.

## Understand
LA is the center of the entertainment industry. The city is spread out and car-dependent. It offers beaches, mountains, deserts, and urban attractions all nearby.

## Get in
Los Angeles International Airport (LAX) is the main gateway. Burbank and Long Beach airports also serve the city. Car rentals and public transit available.

## Get around
A car is essential for covering distances. Metro rail and buses exist but coverage is limited. Ride-sharing services are popular.

## See
- Hollywood Sign: Iconic landmark on hillside
- Griffith Observatory: Free observatory with city views
- Santa Monica Pier: Amusement park on beach
- Getty Center: World-class art museum with architecture
- Venice Beach: Bohemian beach with boardwalk
- Universal Studios: Theme park and working movie studio
- Hollywood Walk of Fame: Sidewalk with star plaques

## Do
- Visit film studios and movie locations
- Hike Runyon Canyon
- Enjoy beaches and beach volleyball
- Shop on Rodeo Drive
- Experience nightlife in various neighborhoods
- Visit museums

## Eat
LA has diverse international cuisine. Try Vietnamese pho, Korean BBQ, Mexican tacos, Japanese ramen, and craft restaurants. Food trucks are excellent.

## Budget
A 3-day trip can cost $800-1600.
