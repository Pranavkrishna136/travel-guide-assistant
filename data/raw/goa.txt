# Goa Travel Guide

Goa, India's smallest state, is known for beaches, Portuguese colonial architecture, and relaxed lifestyle.

## Understand
Goa is a beach destination with Portuguese heritage. The state has a unique culture blend of Indian and Portuguese influences. It's known for its liberal attitude and party scene.

## Get in
Dabolim Airport serves Goa. Trains connect to other Indian cities. Buses available from Mumbai and Bangalore.

## Get around
Taxis and auto-rickshaws common. Motorbike rentals cheap. Buses available. Some walking on beaches.

## See
- Baga Beach: Popular beach with water sports
- Calangute Beach: Busy beach with shops and restaurants
- Arambol Beach: Calm beach in North Goa
- Basilica of Bom Jesus: Church with Xavier's remains
- Se Cathedral: Historic church
- Fort Aguada: Portuguese fort with views
- Dudhsagar Waterfall: Scenic waterfall (day trip)
- Spice plantations: Tours of spice farms

## Do
- Beach relaxation and swimming
- Water sports: parasailing, jet ski
- Visit churches and heritage sites
- Yoga and wellness retreats
- Night parties and clubs
- Boat cruises
- Spice plantation tours

## Eat
Goan cuisine features seafood and coconut. Try fish curry, prawn masala, vindaloo, and coconut rice. Feni (local alcohol) popular. Fresh seafood everywhere.

## Budget
A 3-day trip can cost $200-400.
