# Chiang Mai Travel Guide

Chiang Mai, Thailand's cultural capital, is known for temples, mountains, and relaxed atmosphere.

## Basic Travel Information
Chiang Mai is Thailand's largest northern city. Known for temples, mountain scenery, and Buddhist culture. More relaxed than Bangkok.

## Tourist Attractions
- Old City: Historic walled city with temples
- Wat Chedi Luang: Ancient temple with massive stupa
- Wat Phra Singh: Historic temple
- Wat Chiang Man: Northern Thailand's oldest temple
- Sunday Walking Street: Night market on Sundays
- Saturday Night Market: Antiques and crafts
- Elephant sanctuaries: Day trips with ethical operators
- Doi Suthep: Mountain temple with views

## Best Time to Visit
- November-February: Cool and dry
- March-May: Hot and dry
- June-October: Rainy season

## Transportation Options
- Tuk-tuks: Main local transport
- Songthaews: Red shared trucks
- Motorcycles for rent
- Taxis and ride-sharing apps
- Walking in old city

## Cultural Information
- Buddhist temples everywhere
- Respect for monks and religion
- Friendly and welcoming locals
- Artistic and craft traditions
- Meditation and spiritual culture
- Northern Thai traditions

## Safety Guidelines
- Very safe for tourists
- Petty theft in markets possible
- Motorcycles require caution
- Respect temple customs
- Dress modestly in temples
- Use trusted guides for activities

## Dining
- Northern Thai cuisine unique
- Khao soi: Chiang Mai specialty noodles
- Fresh local vegetables
- Sticky rice traditional
- Street food and night markets
- Coffee culture growing

## Budget
A 3-day trip costs $150-300.
