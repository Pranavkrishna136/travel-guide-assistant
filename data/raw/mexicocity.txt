# Mexico City Travel Guide

Mexico City, the capital of Mexico, is one of the largest metropolitan areas globally with rich history, art, and cuisine.

## Understand
Mexico City sits in a valley and was built by Aztecs. The city blends pre-Hispanic, colonial, and modern architecture. It's a vibrant cultural hub with world-class museums.

## Get in
Mexico City International Airport is the main gateway. Buses connect to other Mexican cities. Trains available to some destinations.

## Get around
The metro system is extensive and cheap. Buses are comprehensive. Taxis and ride-sharing available. Walking in certain neighborhoods is great.

## See
- Templo Mayor: Aztec temple ruins in city center
- Palacio Nacional: Government palace with Diego Rivera murals
- Frida Kahlo Museum: House-museum of the famous artist
- National Museum of Anthropology: World-class museum
- Chapultepec Castle: Historic castle with views
- Xochimilco: UNESCO site with floating gardens and boats
- Mexico City Metropolitan Cathedral: Historic church

## Eat
Mexican cuisine is diverse and delicious. Try tacos, enchiladas, mole, tamales, and street food. Fresh juice stands everywhere. Mezcal and tequila local.

## Budget
A 3-day trip can cost $400-800.
