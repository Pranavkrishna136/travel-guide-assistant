# Kyoto Travel Guide

Kyoto, Japan's former capital, is known for temples, gardens, traditional culture, and geisha districts.

## Understand
Kyoto has over 1,000 years of history. It's the cultural heart of Japan with thousands of temples and shrines. The city preserves traditional Japanese culture.

## Get in
Kansai International Airport serves Kyoto. Shinkansen (bullet train) connects to Tokyo and Osaka. Buses available.

## Get around
Buses are the main transport. Trains connect to surrounding areas. Walking and biking great for exploring. Taxis available.

## See
- Fushimi Inari Shrine: Thousands of red torii gates
- Arashiyama Bamboo Grove: Famous bamboo forest
- Kinkaku-ji: Golden Pavilion temple
- Gion: Historic geisha district
- Kiyomizu-dera: Ancient temple on hilltop
- Sagano: Scenic district with temples
- Philosopher's Path: Canal-side walking path

## Do
- Temple and shrine visits
- Geisha show performances
- Tea ceremonies
- Bamboo grove walks
- Garden viewing
- Traditional crafts workshops
- Seasonal viewing (cherry blossoms, autumn leaves)

## Eat
Kyoto cuisine is refined and seasonal. Try kaiseki, tofu dishes, matcha (green tea), and traditional sweets. Street food and restaurants abundant.

## Budget
A 3-day trip can cost $400-800.
