# Buenos Aires Travel Guide

Buenos Aires, Argentina's capital, is known as the "Paris of South America" with tango, architecture, and steak.

## Basic Travel Information
Buenos Aires is an elegant, European-influenced city. Vibrant cultural scene with museums, theater, and nightlife. One of South America's most cosmopolitan cities.

## Tourist Attractions
- La Boca neighborhood: Colorful buildings and tango culture
- Teatro Colón: Grand opera house
- Casa Rosada: Pink presidential palace
- San Telmo: Historic neighborhood with antique markets
- Recoleta Cemetery: Where Eva Perón is buried
- MALBA Museum: Latin American art museum
- Obelisco: Monument in city center
- Puerto Madero: Revitalized waterfront

## Best Time to Visit
- March-May: Autumn, pleasant weather
- September-November: Spring, mild weather
- December-February: Summer, hot and humid
- June-August: Winter, cool but manageable

## Transportation Options
- Subte (metro): Extensive, affordable system
- Buses: Cheap and everywhere
- Taxis and ride-sharing
- Walking in central neighborhoods
- Commuter trains

## Cultural Information
- Tango is iconic and everywhere
- Strong European influence
- Passionate about football (soccer)
- Literary and artistic culture
- Warm and friendly people
- Café culture strong

## Safety Guidelines
- Generally safe in tourist areas
- Avoid certain neighborhoods at night
- Watch for pickpockets in crowds
- Use official taxis or apps
- Don't flash valuables
- Robbery can occur in poor areas

## Dining
- Steaks are world-class (asados/barbecue)
- Empanadas and pastries
- Fresh pasta and Italian influence
- Wine from local regions excellent
- Street food and cafes
- Choripán (sausage sandwich) popular

## Budget
A 3-day trip costs $300-600.
