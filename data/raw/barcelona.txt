# Barcelona Travel Guide

Barcelona, the capital of Catalonia, is a vibrant Mediterranean city known for its unique architecture, beaches, and cultural heritage.

## Understand
Barcelona is famous for its modernist architecture, particularly the works of Antoni Gaudí. The city has a bohemian, artistic atmosphere with a mix of beaches, urban areas, and mountains. It's known for its progressive culture and passion for football.

## Get in
Barcelona-El Prat Airport is the main gateway. The city is also connected by trains from other European cities. It's easy to reach by road.

## Get around
The Metro is the best way to travel. Buses and trams are also available. Get a T-Casual ticket for unlimited metro rides. Biking is popular for exploring neighborhoods.

## See
- Sagrada Família: Gaudí's unfinished basilica, UNESCO World Heritage Site
- Park Güell: Colorful park with terraces and mosaics
- Casa Batlló: Modernist building with unique facade
- Gothic Quarter: Medieval old town with narrow streets
- Las Ramblas: Famous tree-lined pedestrian street
- Barcelona Beach: Sandy beaches for swimming and relaxation
- Camp Nou: Stadium of FC Barcelona football club

## Do
- Explore the Gothic Quarter's narrow medieval streets
- Watch a live flamenco performance
- Visit the beaches and waterfront
- Attend a Barcelona football match
- Shop at local boutiques and markets

## Eat
Catalan cuisine is excellent. Try tapas, paella, seafood, and jamón ibérico. Vermouth is popular. Street food and pintxos are affordable and delicious.

## Budget
A 3-day trip can cost $500-1000.
