# Rome Travel Guide

Rome, the capital of Italy, is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities and the capital of the Roman Empire. Known as "The Eternal City," Rome is filled with history, art, and culture.

## Understand
Rome is home to over 2,800 years of history. Every corner reveals ancient ruins, Renaissance art, and baroque fountains. The Vatican City is an independent city-state within Rome.

## Get in
Leonardo da Vinci-Fiumicino Airport (FCO) is the main airport. Trains connect Rome to other Italian and European cities. The city is accessible by car.

## Get around
The metro system is efficient and affordable. Buses cover the entire city. Walking is great for exploring historic neighborhoods. Taxis are available but can be expensive.

## See
- Colosseum: Ancient amphitheater and symbol of Rome
- Roman Forum: Archaeological site with ruins of temples and buildings
- Pantheon: Ancient temple with largest unsupported dome
- Vatican City: Smallest independent state, home to St. Peter's Basilica
- Sistine Chapel: Famous for Michelangelo's ceiling frescoes
- Trevi Fountain: Baroque fountain with tossing coins tradition
- Spanish Steps: Historic stairway connecting two piazzas

## Do
- Explore catacombs underground
- Take cooking class or food tour
- Ride a Vespa through the city
- Visit art galleries
- Enjoy wine and gelato

## Eat
Italian cuisine is world-renowned. Try pasta, risotto, pizza, gelato, tiramisu, and espresso. Local trattorias offer authentic food.

## Budget
A 3-day trip can cost $600-1200.
