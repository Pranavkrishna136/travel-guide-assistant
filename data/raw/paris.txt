# Paris Travel Guide

Paris, the capital of France, is a major European city and a global center for art, fashion, gastronomy and culture. Its 19th-century cityscape is crisscrossed by wide boulevards and the River Seine. Beyond such landmarks as the Eiffel Tower and the 12th-century, Gothic Notre-Dame cathedral, the city is known for its cafe culture and designer boutiques along the Rue du Faubourg Saint-Honoré.

## Understand
Paris has been one of the world's most important and attractive cities for over 300 years. It has a population of around 2.2 million within its administrative limits, but the Paris agglomeration has a population of around 10.5 million, making it one of the largest metropolitan areas in Europe.

## Get in
Paris is well connected by air, rail, and road. The main airports are Charles de Gaulle (CDG) and Orly (ORY). The city has excellent train connections via Gare du Nord, Gare de Lyon, etc.

## Get around
The Paris Métro is the most efficient way to get around. Buses, trams, and bikes (Vélib') are also available. Walking is pleasant in many areas.

## See
- Eiffel Tower: Iconic iron lattice tower, 324m tall.
- Louvre Museum: World's largest art museum, home to Mona Lisa.
- Notre-Dame Cathedral: Gothic masterpiece on Île de la Cité.
- Montmartre: Historic hill with Sacré-Cœur Basilica.
- Champs-Élysées: Famous avenue with shops and cafes.

## Do
- Stroll along the Seine.
- Visit museums and galleries.
- Enjoy French cuisine at bistros.
- Take a boat cruise.
- Explore neighborhoods like Le Marais.

## Eat
Paris offers world-class dining. Try croissants, escargot, coq au vin, and desserts like crème brûlée. Budget options include falafel in the Marais.

## Drink
Cafes are everywhere. Try café au lait or wine. Nightlife in areas like Bastille.

## Stay safe
Paris is generally safe, but watch for pickpockets in tourist areas. Use common sense.

## Budget
A 3-day trip can cost $500-1000 depending on accommodation and activities.</content>
