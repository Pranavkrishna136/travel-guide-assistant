# Tokyo Extended - Districts & Culture

## Popular Districts
- Shibuya: Trendy, shopping, nightlife
- Shinjuku: Entertainment, nightlife, neon
- Ginza: Luxury shopping and fine dining
- Harajuku: Youth fashion and pop culture
- Akihabara: Electronics and anime
- Asakusa: Traditional old Tokyo
- Ikebukuro: Shopping and entertainment
- Roppongi: Nightlife and expats

## Temples & Shrines
- Senso-ji: Oldest temple, iconic
- Meiji Shrine: Peaceful forest shrine
- Tsukiji Outer Market: Fish market
- Imperial Palace: Historical residence

## Cultural Activities
- Sumo wrestling matches
- Tea ceremonies
- Calligraphy classes
- Karate and martial arts
- Cherry blossom viewing (spring)
- Anime and manga culture
- Video game arcades
- Karaoke entertainment

## Best Time to Visit
- March-April: Cherry blossoms, spring
- October-November: Autumn colors
- May-June: Green, gardens bloom
- July-August: Festivals, hot and humid

## Transportation Safety
- Most efficient train system globally
- Pickpockets on crowded trains
- Punctuality extremely reliable
- Signage bilingual in most areas
- IC cards simplify payment
- Walking very safe

## Cultural Customs
- Remove shoes in temples
- Silence on trains expected
- Bowing greeting custom
- Business cards exchanged formally
- Respect for elders important
- Punctuality highly valued
- Queue culture strong
- Photography restrictions exist

## Dining Etiquette
- Slurping noodles polite
- Chopstick etiquette important
- Tipping not customary
- Saying "itadakimasu" before meals
- Finishing meals appreciated
- Sake serving ritual

## Safety
- Extremely safe city overall
- Petty theft rare
- Earthquakes possible
- Vending machines everywhere
- 24-hour convenience stores (konbini)
- Police boxes for help
