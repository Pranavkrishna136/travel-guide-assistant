# Paris Safety & Culture

## Safety Guidelines
- Generally safe in main areas
- Pickpockets on metro and crowds
- Watch belongings at markets
- Use official taxis or apps
- Avoid certain neighborhoods at night
- Street crime possible in outer areas
- Scams targeting tourists exist
- Emergency number: 112

## Cultural Information
- Artistic and intellectual heritage
- Fashion capital of world
- Wine and food culture central
- Proud of culture and language
- Romantic ambiance famous
- Arts and museums abundant
- Caf√© society important
- Philosophy and literature valued

## Etiquette
- Learn basic French phrases
- Formal greetings important
- Table manners formal
- Dress well (fashion city)
- Smoking still common
- Tipping not customary but appreciated
- Respect for personal space
- Loud noise considered rude

## Dining Customs
- Long meals tradition
- Lunch is main meal
- Bread and cheese staples
- Wine selection important
- Waiters not rushing diners
- Dessert after main course
- Coffee after dinner
- Aperitif before meal

## Shopping Culture
- Luxury brands everywhere
- Sales seasons (winter/summer)
- Quality over quantity valued
- Designer boutiques abundant
- Haggling not common
- Credit cards widely accepted
- Small shops close Sundays/Mondays

## Museums & Culture
- Louvre and major museums
- Free entry certain hours
- Culture passes available
- Theater and opera frequent
- Street artists and performers
- Galleries in Marais and Latin Quarter

## Transportation
- Metro safe and efficient
- Pickpockets on crowded trains
- Buses comprehensive
- Walking wonderful in many areas
- Drivers can be aggressive
- Bike lanes expanding

## Best Neighborhoods
- Marais: Trendy with shops
- Latin Quarter: Students and culture
- Montmartre: Bohemian and artistic
- Le Havre: Elegant and upscale
- Belleville: Hip and multicultural
